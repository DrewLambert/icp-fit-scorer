## 1. Brand Strategy & Positioning

### Brand Essence

**One-Line Summary:**
*"Sharp, sophisticated, systemic — the trusted advisor who sees what others miss."*

### Positioning Statement

Revelate Operations is a **principal-led RevOps consultancy** specializing in legacy Salesforce modernization for **Series B SaaS teams** ($10M-$50M ARR). The practice embeds with teams for 6-16 weeks, diagnoses what's broken, rebuilds revenue architecture incrementally, and hands off documentation so clients own the system.

### Key Differentiators

| Factor | Typical Agency | Revelate |
|--------|---------------|----------|
| Team | Offshore teams, junior devs | 100% principal-led, US-based |
| Timeline | 9-month rebuilds | 6-16 weeks, incremental |
| Approach | Generic playbooks | Deep diagnostic, custom strategy |
| Billing | Opaque, hourly | Transparent, fixed-price packages |
| Deliverables | Code handoff | Complete documentation & ownership transfer |
| Capacity | Unlimited clients | 2-3 clients maximum |

### Brand Promise

> "Finally, someone who gets it."

Visitors should feel understood, not sold to. The brand recognizes their specific pain before offering solutions.

### Core Value Proposition

- **For:** RevOps leaders at Series B SaaS companies drowning in broken systems
- **Who need:** Trustworthy pipeline data without pausing the business
- **Revelate is:** An embedded operator who's "been in the seat"
- **That delivers:** 6-16 weeks to production, principal-only expertise, documentation that lasts

## 2. Target Audience

### Primary Persona: The Drowning Operator

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Title** | VP of RevOps, Director of Sales Ops, or Technical Founder |
| **Company** | Series B SaaS, $10M-$50M ARR |
| **Age** | 30-45 years old |
| **Technical** | Highly technical, lives in Salesforce, Slack, and dashboards |
| **Environment** | Remote or hybrid, often Boston/SF/NYC markets |

### Pain Points

1. **Broken Forecasting** — Board wants accuracy; CRM gives guesses
2. **Manual Data Entry** — Sales team spends 4+ hours/day on manual work
3. **Disconnected Systems** — Lead routing takes 2+ hours through spreadsheets and Slack
4. **Past Disappointments** — Burned by agencies who over-promised and sent junior offshore devs

### What They Value

- **Speed to value** — Not another 9-month project
- **No sales pressure** — They're skeptical of consultants
- **Proof over promises** — Specific metrics, not vague claims
- **Operator credibility** — Someone who's "been in the seat"

### Visitor Types

| Type | Mindset | Goal | Time on Site |
|------|---------|------|--------------|
| **Ready Buyer** | "I need help now" | Book a call fast | <60 seconds |
| **Researcher** | "Is this person legit?" | Build confidence, then book | 3-7 minutes |
| **Skeptic** | "I've been burned before" | Find proof, overcome doubt | 5-12 minutes |

## 3. Brand Personality & Voice

### The Three Pillars

| Pillar | Expression |
|--------|------------|
| **Sharp Strategist** | Sees patterns others miss. Diagnoses root cause, not symptoms. Data-driven but intuitive. |
| **Experienced Operator** | Been in your seat. Knows the pain personally. Empathy through experience. |
| **Modern Technologist** | AI-augmented. Automation-first. Speaks the language of modern SaaS. |

### Voice Characteristics

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Confident** | Knows his worth. Doesn't over-explain or apologize. | "I fix what's broken." |
| **Precise** | Specific, almost uncomfortably accurate | "Your board wants forecast accuracy. Your CRM gives you guesses." |
| **Lean** | Every word earns its place | Short paragraphs, no fluff |
| **Personal** | Drew's face, story, and voice throughout | First-person where appropriate |
| **Warm but Direct** | Approachable, but won't waste your time | No corporate speak |

### Writing Guidelines

**Do:**
- Lead with pain recognition, then solution
- Use specific numbers and timelines
- Write like you're talking to a smart, busy peer
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences max

**Don't:**
- Use urgency tactics ("LIMITED SPOTS!", "ACT NOW!")
- Use generic consulting language ("leverage synergies")
- Over-explain or hedge
- Use exclamation points excessively

### Example Copy Transformation

| Before (Generic) | After (Revelate) |
|-----------------|------------------|
| "We help companies optimize their revenue operations" | "Your board wants forecast accuracy. Your CRM gives you guesses. I fix that." |
| "Our team of experts delivers results" | "150+ implementations. $100M+ Series C experience. Principal-only delivery." |
| "Contact us to learn more!" | "15 minutes. No pitch. Just clarity on what's broken." |

## 4. Emotional Journey

### Journey Map

| Stage | Duration | User Feels | Design Triggers |
|-------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Landing** | 0-5 sec | "This is legit and modern" | Visual sophistication, clean hierarchy |
| **Scanning** | 5-30 sec | "They understand my world" | SaaS-native aesthetic, industry language |
| **Pain Recognition** | 30-60 sec | "That's exactly my problem" | Precision language, specific pain points |
| **Solution Discovery** | 1-2 min | "There's a path forward" | Clear methodology, incremental approach |
| **Proof Review** | 2-4 min | "He's actually done this" | Specific metrics, real outcomes |
| **Decision** | 4-5 min | "Cautiously optimistic" | Low-pressure CTA, clear next step |

### Desired Emotions

| Emotion | Where It Manifests |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Legitimacy** | Visual quality, specific credentials |
| **Recognition** | Pain points that match their exact situation |
| **Relief** | Clear, achievable path forward |
| **Sharpness** | Feeling that this person sees what others miss |

| Avoid | How We Prevent It |
|-------|-------------------|

| "Immature/unprofessional" | No playful illustrations, quirky humor, startup-bro energy |

## 5. Visual Identity System

### Logo

#### Primary Logo

**File:** `logos/revelate-logo-primary.png`

The Revelate logo features a cosmic vortex mark with the "REVELATE" wordmark. The swirling design represents systematic thinking and the revelation of insights from complex data.

**Logo Exception:** The logo is permitted to use gradients and decorative elements that are prohibited elsewhere in the brand system. Logos are contained identity marks with different rules than UI components.

#### Logo Lockups

| Lockup | Description | Use Case |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| **Primary** | Full mark + wordmark | Hero sections, headers, about pages |
| **Icon Only** | Circular mark without text | Favicon, app icons, tight spaces |
| **Wordmark Only** | "REVELATE" text | Horizontal navigation, text-heavy contexts |

*Note: Icon-only and wordmark-only variants to be created as needed.*

#### Clear Space

Minimum padding around the logo = **1/4 of the logo's diameter** on all sides.

```

│                            │
│    ┌──────────────┐        │
│    │              │        │
│    │    LOGO      │  ←1/4d │
│    │              │        │
│    └──────────────┘        │
│           ↑                │
│          1/4d              │
└────────────────────────────┘
```

#### Minimum Sizes

| Context | Minimum Width | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Digital (web/app) | 120px | Full logo |
| Print | 1 inch (25mm) | Full logo |
| Favicon | 32px | Icon only |
| App icon | 48px | Icon only |

#### Background Usage

| Background | Status | Notes |
|------------|--------|-------|
| Dark backgrounds (#09090b or darker) | Preferred | Current logo optimized for dark |
| Light backgrounds | Needs variant | Create light-background version if needed |
| Busy/patterned backgrounds | Avoid | Logo needs clean backdrop |
| Over photography | Avoid | Use solid overlay first |

#### Logo Don'ts

- Do not stretch or distort the proportions
- Do not add drop shadows, glows, or effects
- Do not change the colors
- Do not rotate or flip
- Do not place on busy backgrounds without contrast
- Do not recreate or modify the vortex design
- Do not use low-resolution versions

## 6. Typography System

### Font Pairing

| Role | Font | Fallback | Weight | Why |
|------|------|----------|--------|-----|
| **Headlines** | Fraunces | Georgia, serif | 600 (Semibold) | Soft serifs add warmth without being stuffy. Variable font with optical sizing. |
| **Body** | Inter | system-ui, sans-serif | 400 (Regular) | The workhorse of modern SaaS. Highly legible for UI and long-form. |
| **Mono** | JetBrains Mono | monospace | 400 | For code snippets, technical details |

### Type Scale

| Token | Size | Line Height | Weight | Usage |
|-------|------|-------------|--------|-------|
| `--text-display` | 64px / 4rem | 1.1 | 600 | Hero headlines |
| `--text-h1` | 48px / 3rem | 1.15 | 600 | Page titles |
| `--text-h2` | 36px / 2.25rem | 1.2 | 600 | Section headers |
| `--text-h3` | 28px / 1.75rem | 1.25 | 600 | Subsection headers |
| `--text-h4` | 22px / 1.375rem | 1.3 | 600 | Card titles |
| `--text-body-lg` | 18px / 1.125rem | 1.6 | 400 | Lead paragraphs |
| `--text-body` | 16px / 1rem | 1.6 | 400 | Body copy |
| `--text-body-sm` | 14px / 0.875rem | 1.5 | 400 | Secondary text |
| `--text-caption` | 12px / 0.75rem | 1.4 | 500 | Labels, meta |
| `--text-overline` | 12px / 0.75rem | 1.4 | 600 | Section labels, uppercase |

### Typography Rules

1. **Headlines in Fraunces** — All H1-H4, display text, hero statements
2. **Body in Inter** — All paragraphs, descriptions, UI text
3. **Generous line-height** — 1.5-1.6 for body, breathing room
4. **Strategic weight contrast** — Semibold (600) for headlines, regular (400) for body
5. **Uppercase sparingly** — Only for overlines/section labels like "WHY CHOOSE DREW"
6. **Text balance** — Use `text-wrap: balance` for headlines to prevent awkward breaks

### Responsive Typography

| Element | Mobile | Desktop | Scale Factor |
|---------|--------|---------|--------------|
| Display headline | 36px | 64px | 0.56x |
| H1 | 30px | 48px | 0.63x |

## 7. Iconography

### Icon Library

**Source:** Lucide Icons (https://lucide.dev)

Lucide was selected for Revelate because:
- **2px stroke weight** — Matches brand's preference for clarity over decoration
- **24px base grid** — Consistent with our 4px spacing system (24 = 6 × 4)
- **MIT licensed** — No attribution required
- **Active maintenance** — Regular updates, good accessibility support
- **Framework agnostic** — Works with React, Vue, or vanilla

### Icon Style Rules

| Attribute | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Style | Outlined only | Filled icons feel heavy; outlined = editorial |
| Stroke weight | 2px (default) | Consistent with Lucide defaults |
| Corner radius | 2px on strokes | Soft but not rounded |
| Color | Single color | Never multicolor or gradient |

### Icon Sizing

| Context | Size | Example Use |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Inline with text | 16px | External link indicator, inline status |
| Button icons | 20px | Icon + label buttons |
| Standalone UI | 24px | Navigation items, card icons |
| Empty states | 48px | Single focal icon on empty views |
| Hero decorative | 64px | Rare; only when icon IS the content |

### When to Use Icons

Icons should **clarify**, not decorate:

- **Navigation items** — Sparingly, one icon per nav section max
- **Button actions** — When text alone is ambiguous (arrow-right for "Next", external-link for outbound)
- **Status indicators** — Check, x, alert-triangle for clear semantic meaning
- **Empty states** — Single hero icon to humanize an empty view
- **Form feedback** — Check for valid, x for error (always paired with text)

### When NOT to Use Icons

This is the critical distinction for Revelate:

- **List item bullets** — Use plain bullets or no bullets; icons clutter
- **Every card header** — Cards don't need decorative icons
- **Decoration or "visual interest"** — If it's not functional, remove it
- **When text alone is clear** — "Save" doesn't need a disk icon
- **Alongside every feature** — Resist the urge to icon-ify everything

### Icon Color

| State | Color Token | Usage |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| Default | `--text-tertiary` | Subtle, doesn't compete with content |
| Hover | `--text-secondary` | Slightly more prominent on interaction |
| Active/Accent | `--accent-primary` | CTAs, active navigation, selected states |
| Disabled | `--text-muted` | Clearly inactive |

**Rule:** Icons inherit text color by default. Only use accent color for interactive/active states.

### Icon Accessibility

| Requirement | Implementation |
|-------------|----------------|
| Decorative icons | `aria-hidden="true"` — screenreader ignores |
| Meaningful icons | `aria-label="Description"` — screenreader announces |
| Icon-only buttons | Always include `aria-label` on the button |
| Icon + text | Icon is decorative; text provides meaning |

These Lucide icons align well with Revelate's technical, RevOps context:

## 10. UX Patterns & Consistency

### Button Rules

1. **One primary per viewport** — If two buttons are near each other, one is primary, one is secondary
2. **Cyan means action** — Don't use cyan for non-interactive elements nearby
3. **Verbs first** — "Book a Call" not "Call Booking"
4. **No icons unless essential** — Arrow icons (→) okay for "forward" actions

### Feedback Patterns

#### Success States
- Toast notification, top-right, auto-dismiss 4s
- Confirmation screen with next steps for major actions

#### Error States
- Inline error below field, immediate
- Toast notification for submission failures

#### Loading States
- Subtle progress bar at top
- Button shows spinner, disabled state
- Skeleton placeholders for content

### Form Patterns

- Single column layout
- Field spacing: 24px between fields
- Section spacing: 32px between groups
- Max width: 480px for forms
- Labels above inputs
- Validation on blur
- Minimum input height on mobile: 48px

### Animation Principles

| Type | Duration | Easing | Usage |
|------|----------|--------|-------|
| Fade in | 300ms | ease-out | Content on scroll |
| Hover transitions | 150ms | ease | Buttons, cards |
| Modal open | 200ms | ease-out | Dialogs appearing |
| Slide | 200ms | ease-out | Mobile drawer |

**Forbidden Animations:**
- Bouncing
- Shaking
- Parallax scrolling
- Auto-playing carousels
- Attention-seeking pulses

### Hover & Focus States

| Element | Hover | Focus |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Buttons | Brightness shift + subtle shadow | 2px accent ring, 2px offset |
| Links | Underline appears | 2px accent ring |
| Cards | Border brightens, subtle lift | 2px accent ring |
| Nav items | Background subtle fill | 2px accent ring |
| Inputs | — | Accent border + ring glow |

## 11. User Journey Flows

### Homepage Section Flow

```
[HERO] → [PAIN RECOGNITION] → [THE DIFFERENCE] → [PROCESS] → [PROOF] → [DREW] → [FINAL CTA]
```

### Site Structure

```
Homepage
├── Hero (pain + promise + CTA)
├── Credibility bar (metrics or logos)
├── Pain recognition (3 problems)
├── The Difference (comparison)
├── Process (3 phases)
├── Proof (case study metrics)
├── About Drew (summary + link)
├── FAQ (4-5 questions, accordion)
└── Final CTA

Services → About → Book
```

### Journey Optimization Principles

1. **Every page has one job** — Homepage converts, Services informs, About builds trust
2. **CTA is never more than one scroll away** — Sticky nav on desktop
3. **No dead ends** — Every page leads to booking
4. **Depth is optional** — Homepage works standalone
5. **Reduce choices** — Fewer nav items, clearer hierarchy

## 15. Prohibited Patterns

These patterns are explicitly forbidden in Revelate brand materials. They represent common AI defaults and generic design choices that undermine brand positioning.

### Visual Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Why It's Prohibited | Instead Do |
|---------|---------------------|------------|
| **Left-side colored borders** | Generic AI/SaaS aesthetic, overused | Use full card backgrounds or subtle bottom borders only |
| **Outlined components with matching fill** | Low contrast, flat, lifeless | Use solid fills with proper contrast or no fill at all |
| **Icon bullets on every list item** | Cluttered, distracting, juvenile | Plain bullets or no bullets; icons only for navigation |
| **Decorative blob shapes** | Generic, startup-bro aesthetic | Clean geometric shapes or nothing |
| **Heavy drop shadows on cards** | Floaty, ungrounded, dated | Subtle shadows + clear borders for architectural feel |
| **Gradient color washes** | Generic tech aesthetic | Solid colors; gradients only in ambient orbital backgrounds |
| **Perfectly symmetrical grids** | Predictable, templated feel | Introduce intentional asymmetry and visual hierarchy |

### Content Anti-Patterns

| Pattern | Why It's Prohibited | Instead Do |
|---------|---------------------|------------|
| **Emojis** | Unprofessional, cheapens premium positioning | Never use. Period. |
| **Exclamation points in headlines** | Overeager, salesy | Confident periods or no punctuation |
| **"We" language** | Corporate, impersonal | "I" or direct statements |
| **Buzzwords** | "Leverage," "synergize," "optimize" feel hollow | Specific, plain language |
| **Stock photo energy** | Staged handshakes, diverse teams pointing at screens | Abstract, data-forward, or no imagery |

### Component Anti-Patterns

| Component | Never Do |
|-----------|----------|
| **Cards** | Left borders, outlined with matching fill, excessive shadows, cramped padding |
| **Buttons** | Gradient fills, icons on every button, excessive border-radius (>4px), ALL CAPS |
| **Callouts/Alerts** | Left border accents, emoji icons, colored backgrounds with colored borders |
| **Tables** | Heavy zebra striping, colored header backgrounds, excessive cell padding |
| **Navigation** | Icon for every nav item, dropdown menus for simple sites, hamburger on desktop |
| **Headers** | Centered everything, decorative underlines, icon prefixes |

## 16. Brand Signatures

These are the distinctive visual treatments that make designs recognizably Revelate. They are deliberately unusual enough that AI will not default to them.

### Signature 1: Editorial Asymmetry

**What:** Layouts that feel curated and intentional, not templated.

**How:**
- Favor 60/40 or 70/30 splits over 50/50
- Let content dictate layout, not grids
- One dominant element, supporting elements orbit it
- Asymmetric whitespace (more on one side than the other)

**Example:** A section with text on the left (60%) and a supporting metric card on the right (40%), with the metric card positioned high rather than centered.

### Signature 2: Precision Typography

**What:** Headlines that feel like statements, not labels.

**How:**
- Headlines in Fraunces always feel like a single thought
- Generous whitespace above and below
- Never cramped, never competing
- Use text-wrap: balance to prevent awkward breaks

**Example:** "Fix your Salesforce. Close more deals." — Two short sentences. Breathing room. Confidence.

### Signature 3: Cyan as Punctuation

**What:** Accent color used like punctuation in typography—sparingly, precisely, meaningfully.

**How:**
- CTAs (one per viewport)
- Focus rings
- Subtle metric highlights
- Link hover states
- Never as fills, backgrounds, or decoration

**Example:** In a page with 20 elements, cyan appears in exactly 2-3 places.

### Signature 4: Solid, Grounded Cards

**What:** Cards that feel architectural and stable, never floaty.

**How:**
- Subtle shadow (not heavy)
- Clear, light borders (not colored)
- Solid fills (never transparent)
- Consistent padding (24px)
- Connects visually to the surface it sits on

### Signature 5: Data-Forward Aesthetics

**What:** Specificity and metrics over decoration.

**How:**
- Lead with numbers when available
- Tables and structured data over paragraphs
- Precise language over marketing fluff
- Whitespace around metrics to let them breathe

**Example:** "150+ implementations" not "Extensive experience." A clean table of deliverables, not a paragraph describing them.

### Signature 6: Negative Space as Design

**What:** Generous whitespace is not empty—it's intentional.

**How:**
- Resist filling every gap
- Section padding: 96px minimum (desktop)
- Headlines get room to breathe
- Let quality content stand alone

**Example:** A hero section with just headline + subhead + button. No decorative elements filling space.

### Signature 7: Monospace Moments

**What:** JetBrains Mono appears at strategic moments to signal technical credibility.

**How:**
- Code snippets
- Technical specs or data labels
- Form field placeholders (when technical)
- Never for body copy or headlines

**Example:** A metric card showing "6-16 weeks" with the number in monospace.
